# Potential Contributions
Spearmint can only go as far as the knowledge of the people involved in its creation.
While Spearmint seeks to be a comprehensive database, that takes contributions from many people from many different areas.

Once an individual submits an approved contribution, I will restructure this document to use first-person **plural** pronouns.

As the sole contributor for the time being, I do love to learn and am always seeking to further my understanding of the world.
That being said, I not only accept corrections but welcome them wholeheartedly.

Constructive criticism is critical to better not only ourselves, but the work we put forward.
In that spirit, see below for guidelines that all contributors (including myself) must meet.

# Spearmint Guidelines
## Be Transparent
When a document has the primary intent of education (tutorial, walkthrough, guide, etc.), some underlying foundations may not be evident in the writing.
A direct quote, paraphrase, testing material, experience, bias, and anything that was used to create the document should be listed within the document itself.

This commonly takes place with the use of `footnotes`.
In markdown documents (`*.md`), this is through the use of `[^#]` at the place of mention and `[^#]` at the footnote's intended location.

## Consistently Revise
Information changes all the time. When this is brought to my attention, I will edit the document to incorporate that updated information (along with some comment within either the document or surrounding container).

These updates do not need to happen every single second this information is brought to light.
A good practice would be to comment within the document or surrounding container the information that was discovered and that it is yet to be implemented into appropriate formatting.

## Take Your Time
None of this is a rush, race, or other speedrunning contest.
The only work that will be accepted is that which is comprehensive and complete, unless otherwise specified.

Cite your sources, pay attention to differing opinions, take heed of what didn't work for you but did for someone else.
The goal of Spearmint is to make sure information is freely accessible in a format that incorporates everyone's level of education.

## Put in the Work
I do not and will not accept contributions that are not attributed properly.
There's no reason to incorporate a document into Spearmint that contains false, inaccurate, or otherwise misleading information.

I vehemently dislike the use of Generative AI (GenAI) and am going to stand firmly on that stance.
Contributors may use GenAI, as long as the author's voice is apparent and all guidelines are followed.

The expectation is that all authors/contributors are able to fully stand by their work, decisions, and explanations.
At times, those reasons may be akin to "I'm unsure", "This is my best guess", "From what I could tell", etc.
Those reasons are fully acceptable, as long as the guidelines are followed.

## Enjoy It
Don't contribute if it is not enjoyable.
Frankly, I don't want your contribution if you don't enjoy it.

When you don't enjoy contributing, it likely means you won't seek to keep it updated or improve when requested.
Spearmint is meant to be an ever-evolving and updated database, future individuals flocking here deserve the respect we would want in their scenario.
